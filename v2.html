V2 Missile (1944)

Rocket-Powered Ballistic Missile

Bombs Raining from the Sky

Description and Major Uses

The German V-2 rocket was the world's first large-scale liquid-propellant rocket vehicle, the first long-range ballistic missile, and the ancestor of today's large rockets and launch vehicles. Launched from mobile platforms, the missile had a maximum range of about 320 km (200 miles) and a one-ton warhead. From September 1944 to March 1945, Germany managed to launch more than 3 000 ballistic missiles at the cities of Britain, Belgium, France and the Netherlands. If the anti-aircraft missile system quickly managed to track and shoot down V-1 missiles, it was powerless against ballistics. And As a result of the attack on the town of Tewkesbury in central England, almost 900 people were left without homes, on January 20, 1945. (Pictured Right)

The Nazis created the V2 missile during World War II to counteract the air defense systems of the British. Since the V2 could fly much higher than any previous missiles, it was ideal for dropping bombs from above the range of radar systems and maintain the element of surprise.

However the V2 missile had several technical issues and failures during its development which prevented it from seeing much success in the war. Although it was a technological marvel, and its impact was limited in WW2 it paved the way for future rocket ship technology, as one V2 missile reached the edge of space.
