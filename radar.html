Radar

Radio Detection And Ranging Systems

Featured Technologies

Description and Major Uses

In the early 20th century, Radar (standing for Radio Detection And Ranging) systems were used in many different nations in an attempt to detect incoming aircrafts, the most popular of which was the Chain Home radar developed in Britain. The Chain Home radar or the “Chain Home” network became operational by September 1938. It was the first early-warning radar network used for air-defense. The Chain Home radars allowed Britain to successfully deploy its limited air defenses against the heavy German air attacks conducted during the early part of the war.

Origin and Creator

The history of radar started with experiments by Heinrich Hertz in the late 19th century that showed that radio waves were reflected by metallic objects. On February 26th 1935, Watson-Watt and Arnold Wilkins successfully demonstrated their system using a BBC transmitter, and managed to pick up a Handley Page Heyford bomber being used as a test target. Robert Watson-Watt believed that radio beams could be bounced off enemy aircraft to detect them. He asked his assistant, Arnold F “Skip” Wilkins, to undertake calculations to demonstrate the feasibility of ‘aircraft detection by radio waves’.
